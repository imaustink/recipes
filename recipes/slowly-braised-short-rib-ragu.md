# Slowly Braised Short Rib Ragu

## Ingredients

### Slowly Braised Short Rib Ragu Sauce
1. 4 pounds English cut beef short ribs
2. 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
3. 3 large carrots, peeled and diced
4. 1 large yellow onion, finely diced
5. 1 stalk celery, finely diced
6. 8 cloves garlic, finely chopped or grated
7. 6 ounces tomato paste
8. 1 cup dry red wine
9. 1 package fresh 'poultry herb blend' (or approx. 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, 4 sprigs fresh sage leaves and 12 sprigs fresh thyme)
10. 2 bay leaves
11. optional: 1 parmesan rind
12. 14 ounces crushed tomatoes
13. 2 cups low-sodium beef broth/stock or water
14. kosher salt and ground black pepper, to season

### Short Rib Ragu Pasta
1. 24 ounces pappardelle or pasta of choice, such as casarecce, bucatini, or gnocchi
2. ½ cup heavy cream
3. ½ cup grated parmesan
4. for serving, as desired: grated parmesan, finely chopped fresh herbs, etc.

## Directions

### Braised Short Rib Ragu Sauce
1. Add olive oil to a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Pat the beef short ribs dry and season with kosher salt and ground black pepper.
2. Once the oil is hot, add the short ribs to the pot. Work in batches to avoid overcrowding. Cook for 3-4 minutes per side until browned. Transfer to a plate and set aside.
3. Drain off all but 2 tablespoons of drippings from the pot. Return the pot to medium heat.
4. Add the soffritto (carrots, onion, and celery) to the pot, seasoning with kosher salt and ground black pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until deeply browned, 15-20 minutes.
5. Add garlic to the pot with the soffritto. Cook until fragrant, 1-2 minutes. Add tomato paste, stirring to coat the soffritto. Cook 2-3 minutes until browned.
6. Increase heat to medium-high, pour red wine into the pot. Stir constantly, scraping up any browned bits. Cook for 3-4 minutes until wine is almost absorbed.
7. Tie herbs together using kitchen twine, then add to the pot with bay leaves and parmesan rind (if using). Add crushed tomatoes, beef broth/stock, and browned short ribs. Stir to combine.
8. Bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer, stirring occasionally, for 2 1/2 – 3 hours, or until short ribs are fall-apart tender.
9. Transfer short ribs to a plate or cutting board. Remove and discard herbs, bay leaves, and parmesan rind from the pot. Shred short ribs into bite-sized pieces, discarding bones. Return shredded short ribs to the pot. Stir to combine.

### Short Rib Ragu Pasta
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add pasta and cook until al dente according to package directions. Reserve about 1 cup of starchy pasta water, then drain the pasta.
2. Bring braised short rib ragu up to a simmer. Stir in heavy cream and parmesan cheese. Continue to simmer over low heat, stirring occasionally.
3. Add cooked pasta to the pot with the braised short rib ragu sauce, tossing to coat. Add reserved pasta water if needed to loosen the ragu; add extra parmesan if needed to tighten it. Cook over medium heat for 1-2 minutes.
4. Portion short rib ragu pappardelle into individual pasta bowls, topping with additional grated parmesan, chopped fresh herbs, and/or crushed red pepper as desired. Serve immediately.

## Equipment

1. Large heavy-bottomed pot
2. Paper towels
3. Wooden spoon
4. Tongs or 2 forks
5. Liquid measuring cup

## Tips

1. For maximum flavor, look for meaty short ribs with nice marbling.
2. Use kitchen twine to tie herbs together for easy removal.
3. Reserve some starchy pasta water to adjust sauce consistency.
