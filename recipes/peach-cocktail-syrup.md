# Peach Cocktail Syrup

## Ingredients

1. 2 cups fresh or frozen peaches, peeled and diced (about 3-4 medium peaches)
2. 1 cup granulated sugar
3. 1 cup water
4. 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
5. 1 vanilla bean, split (or 1 teaspoon vanilla extract)

## Directions

1. Combine the diced peaches, sugar, and water in a medium saucepan.
2. Scrape the seeds from the vanilla bean into the pan and drop in the pod (or set the vanilla extract aside to add later).
3. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally to dissolve the sugar.
4. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 15-20 minutes, until the peaches are very soft and the liquid has reduced slightly.
5. Remove from heat and stir in the lemon juice (and vanilla extract, if using instead of a vanilla bean).
6. Let the mixture cool for 10 minutes, then strain through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl or jar, pressing on the solids to extract as much syrup as possible.
7. Discard the solids (or save them for topping yogurt or ice cream) and the vanilla pod.
8. Transfer the syrup to a sealable bottle or jar and refrigerate until fully chilled.

## Equipment

1. Medium saucepan
2. Fine-mesh sieve
3. Sealable bottle or jar

## Tips

Use about 1-2 ounces of this syrup to sweeten and flavor cocktails like a peach bellini, peach whiskey sour, or peach mule - it also works well in sparkling water or iced tea for a non-alcoholic drink. Store in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks. For a deeper flavor, use very ripe or slightly overripe peaches.
